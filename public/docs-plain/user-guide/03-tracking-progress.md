## Training Units Explained

### What Are Training Units?

TU is MuscleMap's proprietary metric for measuring workout volume.

```
Traditional:  100 bicep curls = 100 reps
              100 squats      = 100 reps
              (Same score for VERY different work!)

MuscleMap:    100 bicep curls ≈ 35 TU
              100 squats      ≈ 180 TU
              (Reflects actual training stimulus!)
```

### TU Calculation

```
TU = Sum of (muscle_volume x activation% x difficulty)

Where:
- muscle_volume: Relative size of each muscle group
- activation%: How much the muscle is engaged (0-100%)
- difficulty: Exercise complexity multiplier
```

### Muscle Volume Factors

| Muscle Group | Relative Size | TU Multiplier |
|--------------|---------------|---------------|
| Quadriceps | Large | 1.0 |
| Glutes | Large | 0.9 |
| Hamstrings | Medium-Large | 0.7 |
| Back (Lats) | Large | 0.8 |
| Chest | Medium | 0.6 |
| Shoulders | Medium | 0.5 |
| Core | Medium | 0.4 |
| Triceps | Small-Medium | 0.35 |
| Biceps | Small | 0.3 |
| Calves | Small | 0.25 |
| Forearms | Small | 0.2 |

### Why TU Matters

- **Fair comparison** across different workout types
- **Balanced training** naturally encouraged
- **Real progress** tracking, not just showing up
- **Better recommendations** for your next workout

### 1RM Calculation

MuscleMap estimates your one-rep max using the Epley formula:

```
1RM = weight × (1 + reps/30)

Example:
- You lift 80kg for 8 reps
- 1RM = 80 × (1 + 8/30) = 80 × 1.27 = 101 kg
```
